What is HygroSphere?

HygroSphere is a passive water system that pulls moisture from the air and then disinfects it using an accelerated form of solar disinfection, combining both steps in a single device without needing electricity or added chemicals.

Who we are

HygroSphere began with a journey through Rajasthan's dry heartland. On a school trip to the Aravalli hills, we saw villages where handpumps stood dry and queues lasted hours beside water tankers for a few pots of water. We understood that this wasn't just an exception, but rather a pattern across Rajasthan. Due to desertification, millions across Rajasthan have no idea where their next sip would come from. With groundwater levels falling by over a metre each year, and unreliable grid power that renders electric water systems ineffective, these low-income communities lack a viable solution that addresses their specific needs and conditions.

As students passionate about sustainability, and social impact, this question puzzled us: what does a water solution look like when electricity, infrastructure, and monetary resources are sparse? That very question, then, brought us together over late-night brainstorming sessions that turned into designs, experiments, and, finally, a prototype.

Over months of iteration, failures, and refining, an idea finally took shape - a system that leveraged Rajasthan's climate to provide exactly what these communities needed - reliable, potable and clean water. HygroSphere emerged as an electricity-free water solution that combines atmospheric water harvesting with solar disinfection, designed specifically for arid and semi-arid regions. What started as a student project has now grown into a viable solution for billions around the world facing water scarcity.
